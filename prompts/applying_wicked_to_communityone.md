# Applying *Wicked* to CommunityOne

Whether “CommunityOne” refers to a specific social platform, a corporate initiative, or a grassroots movement, the themes of *Wicked* provide a sharp lens for analysis.

## 1. Radical inclusivity vs. “performative” goodness

In *Wicked*, Glinda represents the “popular” consensus—the face of what the community *thinks* it wants. Elphaba represents the “other.”

**CommunityOne application:** A healthy community must decide if it values the optics of harmony (Glinda) or the reality of diversity (Elphaba). Does CommunityOne embrace those who “look” or “act” different, or does it force them to conform to stay “popular”?

## 2. The power of narrative (who writes the story?)

The Wizard stays in power by controlling the news and painting Elphaba as a villain to unite the citizens against a common enemy.

**CommunityOne application:** In any centralized community, the leadership or the “majority” often controls the narrative. *Wicked* warns us to look past the official “Wicked” labels and seek the truth from marginalized voices within the group.

## 3. Collective responsibility and “the Animals”

The slow silencing of the Animals in Oz happens because most citizens are too busy with their own lives to notice.

**CommunityOne application:** This reflects the importance of advocacy. A community is only as strong as its protection of its most vulnerable members. If CommunityOne aims for unity, it must ensure that unity isn’t achieved by silencing smaller sub-groups or “animals.”

## 4. “Unlimited” potential through collaboration

The most powerful moments in the story occur when Elphaba and Glinda work together, bridging their ideological gaps (“For Good”).

**CommunityOne application:** The ultimate goal of a unified community is the “Defying Gravity” moment—where individual talents are amplified by mutual support, allowing the community to transcend traditional limitations.

---

In short: *Wicked* teaches us that a true community isn’t defined by how well everyone fits in, but by how well it treats those who stand out.

## Two lenses, one story (we use both)

This narrative deliberately combines **community management** and **CommunityOne’s social philosophy**—they shape each other, and *Wicked* pressures both at once.

- **Community management** (moderation, norms, safety, onboarding, escalation, who gets heard in a thread or channel) is where Glinda-like “popularity” and Wizard-like narratives become *daily practice*. Sections 1–3 ask whether rules and culture protect the “other” or polish a comfortable façade.

- **CommunityOne social philosophy** (mission, legitimacy, power, **who gets surfaced** in data, on the map, in search, and in “official” views) is where Oz’s **story** becomes **infrastructure**. Sections 2 and 3 apply directly: defaults, rankings, and “the map” can unify—or can quietly sideline the Animals unless design and governance insist otherwise.

Section 4 is the bridge: collaboration only works when management *and* philosophy align—when mutual support is real policy, not only a tagline. Holding **both** lenses keeps the story honest: neither “just moderation” nor “just mission” alone answers who is still invisible when the lights go up.
